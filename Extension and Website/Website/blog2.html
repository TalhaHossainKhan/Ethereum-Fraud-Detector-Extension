Cryptocurrency Adoption:
Lessons from El Salvador to Bangladesh

Published on June 5, 2023

Cryptocurrencies have become a focal point in global economic discussions, with countries like El Salvador taking the bold step of embracing Bitcoin as legal tender. For Bangladesh, a developing nation, the potential benefits and challenges of such a move cannot be ignored.

El Salvador’s implementation of Bitcoin has substantially increased financial inclusion. With approximately 80% of its population unbanked, Bangladesh could see a significant boost in financial participation. According to recent surveys, up to 87% of Salvadorans now have access to financial services, showcasing the potential impact on Bangladesh’s unbanked population, estimated to be around 50 million.

Bangladesh, akin to El Salvador, heavily relies on remittances, contributing significantly to its GDP. Bitcoin transactions offer substantially lower fees compared to traditional banking methods. By adopting cryptocurrency, Bangladesh could save millions in remittance-related costs, leading to increased disposable income for recipients and potentially bolstering domestic spending.

El Salvador’s Bitcoin adoption has attracted substantial foreign investment in the cryptocurrency sector. With an influx of foreign capital, Bangladesh’s economy could experience significant growth. In the first quarter of Bitcoin’s adoption in El Salvador, the country attracted over $120 million in cryptocurrency investment, a figure that could inspire confidence in potential investors eyeing Bangladesh’s market.

Bitcoin adoption fosters technological innovation. It encourages the development of blockchain-related skills and industries. This innovation could lead to a notable increase in the country’s GDP. Countries investing in blockchain technology have witnessed an average GDP increase of 0.5%, a figure that Bangladesh, aspiring for rapid economic development, must take into consideration.

However, cryptocurrency markets are renowned for their volatility. Bitcoin’s value can fluctuate dramatically within short periods. This instability poses a significant risk for individuals and businesses alike. Bangladesh’s GDP, growing steadily but still vulnerable, might suffer from the unpredictability of cryptocurrency markets.

Establishing effective cryptocurrency regulations is imperative. Without robust regulatory frameworks, Bangladesh could fall prey to illicit activities. Data from El Salvador indicates a rise in cybercrime since Bitcoin’s implementation, highlighting the necessity for stringent regulatory measures in Bangladesh to prevent similar consequences.

Cryptocurrency mining, fundamental to the operation of digital currencies, demands substantial energy resources. Considering Bangladesh’s existing energy challenges, a surge in demand due to mining activities could lead to energy shortages. The country must assess its energy capabilities before diving into widespread cryptocurrency adoption.

Bangladesh faces a significant digital divide. A substantial portion of its population lacks access to the Internet and digital technologies. Cryptocurrency adoption could further isolate marginalized communities, hindering their economic and social progress. Addressing this divide is paramount to ensuring an inclusive digital financial landscape.

In conclusion, while El Salvador’s implementation of cryptocurrency showcases potential benefits in financial inclusion, remittances, economic growth, and technological advancement, Bangladesh must meticulously navigate the challenges. Implementing stringent regulations, addressing energy concerns, and ensuring inclusive access to technology are vital steps toward the successful integration of cryptocurrencies into Bangladesh’s economy.
